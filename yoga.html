Basic Yoga Guide

Discover the foundational yoga poses to improve flexibility, strength, and mental clarity. Perfect for beginners and experienced practitioners alike.

Essential Yoga Poses

Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

Stand tall with feet together, shoulders relaxed, weight evenly distributed through your soles. Arms at sides. Take a deep breath and raise your hands overhead.

Benefits:

  • Improves posture
  • Strengthens thighs, knees, and ankles
  • Relieves sciatica
  • Reduces flat feet

Downward-Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

Start on your hands and knees. Lift your hips up and back, straightening your legs and arms to form an inverted V shape.

Benefits:

  • Calms the brain and relieves stress
  • Strengthens arms and legs
  • Stretches shoulders, hamstrings, and calves
  • Energizes the body

Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)

Stand with feet wide apart. Turn your right foot out 90 degrees and left foot in slightly. Bend your right knee over right ankle.

Benefits:

  • Strengthens legs and ankles
  • Stretches groins, chest and lungs, shoulders
  • Stimulates abdominal organs
  • Increases stamina

Tree Pose (Vrksasana)

Stand straight and shift weight onto left foot. Place right foot on left thigh, keeping hips forward. Bring palms together in front of chest.

Benefits:

  • Improves balance and stability
  • Strengthens thighs, calves, ankles, and spine
  • Stretches groins and inner thighs
  • Relieves sciatica

Child's Pose (Balasana)

Kneel on floor, touch big toes together and sit on heels. Separate knees hip-width apart. Exhale and lay torso between thighs.

Benefits:

  • Gently stretches hips, thighs, and ankles
  • Calms the brain and helps relieve stress
  • Relieves back and neck pain
  • Helps with fatigue

Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)

Lie on your stomach with your hands under your shoulders. Press into your hands to lift your chest off the floor, keeping your hips grounded.

Benefits:

  • Strengthens the spine
  • Stretches chest and lungs, shoulders, and abdomen
  • Firms the buttocks
  • Helps relieve stress and fatigue

Yogic Breathing (Pranayama)

Pranayama is the practice of breath control in yoga. It's a fundamental part of yoga practice that helps to calm the mind and prepare for meditation.

Deep Belly Breathing

Focus on expanding your belly as you inhale deeply through your nose, and contract as you exhale.

Equal Breathing

Inhale for a count of four, then exhale for a count of four. Maintain this equal rhythm.

Alternate Nostril Breathing

Close your right nostril and inhale through the left, then close the left and exhale through the right.
